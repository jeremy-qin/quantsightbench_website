Key Findings

Analysis across 34 model configurations reveals systematic patterns in how LLMs handle uncertainty quantification.

Systematic Underconfidence

All models produce intervals that are too narrow. The best model (GPT-5.1 med) achieves only 78.4% coverage against a 90% target. No model reaches the desired calibration level.

Reasoning Effort Matters

Higher reasoning effort consistently improves coverage across all providers. Opus 4.5 improves from 65.4% (low) to 72.5% (high), while maintaining similar interval quality.

Agentic Retrieval Helps Sharpness

Models with web search access produce sharper intervals (lower median IS: 40-69 vs 70-98), though coverage gains are mixed. Kimi K2 Thinking benefits the most from retrieval augmentation.

Prompt Design Has Large Impact

The informed prompt (with background context) consistently outperforms zero-shot across all models. For GLM-4.7, adding context improves mean log IS from 10.9 to 9.41, a 14% improvement.

No Single Winner

Kimi K2.5 achieves the best mean log IS of 6.33, followed by Opus 4.5 at 6.74. GPT-5.1 leads in coverage at 78.4%. The top models span multiple providers, showing no single dominant approach.

Methodology

1

Dataset

1,000 resolved forecasting questions from Open Foresight (September 2025 snapshot). Questions span diverse domains including economics, politics, science, technology, and society, with numeric outcomes.

2

Task

Each model receives a forecasting question and must produce a 90% prediction interval (lower bound, median, upper bound). The interval should contain the true outcome 90% of the time while being as narrow as possible.

3

Scoring

We use the Interval Score (Gneiting & Raftery, 2007), which rewards narrow intervals and penalizes missed coverage. The log-transformed mean is our primary metric. A penalty is added for invalid/unparseable outputs.

4

Configurations

Models are tested across multiple axes: reasoning effort (low/medium/high), prompt setting (zero-shot vs informed), and retrieval augmentation (standard vs agentic with web search). This yields 34 total configurations.

Metrics Glossary

Metric Description Ideal
Mean Log IS Mean of log-transformed interval scores across all valid predictions. Primary ranking metric. Lower is better
Coverage Fraction of questions where the ground truth falls within the predicted interval. 0.90 (= 90%)

About

QuantSightBench is a benchmark for evaluating the uncertainty quantification capabilities of large language models. Unlike traditional benchmarks that focus on point predictions, QuantSightBench assesses whether models can produce well-calibrated prediction intervals that accurately reflect their epistemic uncertainty.

The benchmark uses real-world forecasting questions with known resolutions, making it a practical test of probabilistic reasoning. All questions come from the Open Foresight platform, ensuring diverse, high-quality evaluation data.
